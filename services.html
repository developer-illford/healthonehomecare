Healthcare Staffing Services for Care Homes

We provide experienced professionals to support the daily operations of care homes and ensure residents receive the highest standard of care.

Registered Nurses

Our registered nurses are experienced in delivering clinical care within care home environments. They support medication administration, health monitoring, care planning, and ensure compliance with healthcare standards.

Healthcare Assistants

Our healthcare assistants provide essential support with personal care, mobility, and daily living activities while maintaining dignity and respect for every resident.

Professional chefs and other kitchen staff

Proper nutrition plays an important role in resident wellbeing. Our chefs and kitchen staff are experienced in preparing nutritious meals while accommodating dietary requirements and maintaining food safety standards.

Domestic Staff

Clean and hygienic environments are essential in care homes. Our domestic staff help maintain high standards of cleanliness and infection control throughout the facility.

Support Workers for Hostels & Supported Living

We supply experienced Support Workers to hostels and supported living facilities, providing care that is compassionate, resilient, and independence focused.

The Role of Our Support Workers

Our staff support individuals experiencing:

Homelessness Mental health challenges Learning disabilities Substance recovery needs Social vulnerability

Their responsibilities include:

Providing practical daily living support Encouraging structure and positive routines Offering emotional reassurance and motivation Supporting attendance at appointments Promoting independence and life skills Maintaining professional boundaries and safeguarding Our aim is to empower individuals to build confidence, stability, and long-term independence.

Support Workers for Hostels and Assisted Living

We provide trained and compassionate Support Workers for hostels, supported accommodation, and assisted living services across Northern Ireland. Our support workers play an essential role in helping service users live as independently as possible while ensuring their safety, wellbeing, and dignity are always maintained.

Our staff understand that individuals in supported living environments often require guidance, encouragement, and structured support to manage daily life. Each support worker is carefully selected and trained to deliver person-centred care that promotes independence, stability, and wellbeing.

Supporting a Wide Range of Needs

Our support workers are experienced in assisting individuals with a variety of needs, including:

Mental health conditions Learning disabilities Autism spectrum conditions Substance misuse recovery Homelessness and housing support Physical disabilities Behavioural challenges Emotional and social support needs Transition from hospital or institutional care to independent living

We work closely with organisations to ensure the right staff are matched to the needs of each service user and environment.

Promoting Independence

Our support workers focus on empowering individuals to develop the skills and confidence needed to live as independently as possible. They provide guidance and encouragement with:

Daily living skills Personal care and hygiene Meal preparation and nutrition Medication prompts and wellbeing monitoring Managing appointments and routines Budgeting and household management Building social connections and community engagement

Emotional and Practical Support

Many individuals in supported accommodation benefit from consistent encouragement and emotional support. Our staff are trained to provide compassionate guidance, active listening, and reassurance, helping service users feel safe, respected, and supported.

Continuity and Reliability

We understand that trust and consistency are vital when working with vulnerable individuals. Wherever possible, we aim to provide regular support workers to ensure continuity of care and to build positive relationships with service users.

Training and Professional Standards

All our support workers undergo a thorough recruitment and training process to ensure they are fully prepared to work in supported living environments. Training may include:

Safeguarding and protection of vulnerable adults Mental health awareness Managing challenging behaviours Health and safety procedures Communication and person-centred care Equality, dignity, and respect in care

1:1 Specialist Care Support

We supply experienced healthcare assistants and registered nurses to private care homes across Northern Ireland, with a strong focus on high-quality one-to-one care

We provide dedicated one-to-one support for residents requiring enhanced supervision, reassurance, or complex care. Our services include:

Continuous supervision to reduce falls risk Dementia support and reassurance Emotional support for anxious or distressed residents Waking night observation End-of-life companionship and care

Our personalised approach enhances safety, emotional wellbeing, and overall quality of life.

Bespoke one to one care packages

Bespoke Care Packages for Residents with Complex Needs

We provide specialist one-to-one (1:1) care packages designed to support residents who require enhanced supervision, personalised attention, and a higher level of care. Our bespoke service allows care homes to safely support residents with complex physical, cognitive, or behavioural needs while maintaining the highest standards of care.

Our dedicated 1:1 care staff are carefully selected and specially trained to provide focused support for residents who cannot safely be supported within standard staffing ratios and ensures continuity of care and meaningful engagement.

Supporting Residents with Higher Care Needs

Our specialist 1:1 care services support residents experiencing a range of complex conditions, including:

Residents at high risk of falls Advanced dementia and cognitive impairment Wandering or exit-seeking behaviours Mental health challenges or suicidal tendencies Anxiety, agitation, or behavioural distress Residents requiring close monitoring for safety Post-hospital recovery requiring additional observation Palliative and end-of-life care support Residents who benefit from consistent reassurance and emotional support

By providing dedicated supervision and support, we help reduce risk, promote safety, and improve the overall wellbeing of residents.

Continuity of care

We recognise that trust and familiarity are essential in providing effective care. Wherever possible, we assign the same trained staff member or small team to each resident. This continuity helps build strong relationships, reduces anxiety for residents, and ensures consistent, person-centred care.

Specialised training for every placement

Each member of our 1:1 care team receives additional training tailored to the specific needs of the resident they support. This may include:

Dementia and behavioural support Mental health awareness Falls prevention and mobility assistance Safeguarding and risk management Communication techniques for residents with cognitive impairment

This specialised preparation ensures that our staff are fully equipped to provide safe, compassionate, and responsive care.

Meaningful Engagement and Activities

Beyond supervision, our carers focus on enhancing residentsâ€™ quality of life through meaningful engagement and activities. Personalised activities help promote mental stimulation, emotional wellbeing, and a sense of purpose.

Examples include:

Gentle exercise and mobility activities Conversation, companionship, and social engagement Reading, puzzles, and memory-based activities Music, arts, and creative hobbies Sensory stimulation for residents with dementia

By encouraging positive engagement, our carers help residents remain active, supported, and emotionally connected.

A Person-Centred Approach

Every resident has unique needs. Our bespoke 1:1 care packages are designed in partnership with care homes to deliver tailored support that prioritises safety, dignity, and wellbeing.

Our goal is simple: to provide skilled, compassionate professionals who make a meaningful difference in the lives of the residents they support. We aim to provide consistent staff to ensure continuity of care and meaningful engagement.

Domiciliary Care Services (Health One Home care)

Supporting independence in the comfort of home

Our Health One Home Care services support individuals who wish to remain independent while receiving care in the comfort of their own homes.

Our carers assist with personal care, daily living tasks, companionship, medication reminders, and specialised support for conditions such as dementia, disability, or recovery after illness.
